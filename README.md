# Mathematical Theory of Radon Transforms

A rigorous mathematical monograph exploring the theory, structure, and analytical foundations of Radon Transforms, written with a focus on clarity, precision, and mathematical depth.

This work aims to present the Radon Transform not merely as an applied tool in imaging and tomography, but as a rich mathematical object with deep connections to integral geometry, harmonic analysis, inverse problems, and partial differential equations.

## About This Book

This monograph covers the mathematical theory behind Radon Transforms, including:

- Definition and foundational properties
- Geometric interpretation
- Functional analytic framework
- Inversion formulas
- Connections with Fourier analysis
- Applications in tomography
- Relations with PDEs and inverse problems
- Theoretical perspectives and advanced results

The objective is to build the subject from first principles while maintaining mathematical rigor throughout.

---

## Why This Work Exists

Many treatments of Radon Transforms focus primarily on engineering applications such as CT scans and medical imaging. While important, this often leaves the deeper mathematical structure underdeveloped.

This work was written to bridge that gap.

The goal is to provide a mathematically rigorous treatment for readers who wish to understand the subject beyond applications and into its analytical core.
